<p><p>Gene editing technologies have revolutionized the field of biotechnology, with three main market types emerging: Double Strand Break Repair, Engineered Nucleases, and Clustered Regularly Interspaced Short Palindromic Repeats (CRISPR). Double Strand Break Repair involves repairing DNA breaks using template DNA. Engineered Nucleases use enzymes to precisely cut and edit DNA sequences. CRISPR technology utilizes RNA molecules to guide enzymes to specific DNA locations for editing. These markets are driving advancements in genetic engineering and personalized medicine.</p></p>
