<p><p>Surgical drainage devices are used to remove excess fluids or air from a surgical site to promote healing. Active drainage devices use negative pressure to actively suction fluids out of the body, while passive drainage devices rely on gravity to allow fluids to drain passively. Active drainage devices are typically used in cases where a large volume of fluid needs to be removed quickly, while passive drainage devices are more commonly used for continuous, slow drainage over a longer period of time.</p></p>
