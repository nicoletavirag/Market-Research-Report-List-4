<p><p>Threaded fasteners refer to screws, nuts, and bolts that have helical ridges or a continuous spiral thread designed to create a secure connection by being twisted or driven into a material. Non-threaded fasteners include rivets, pins, and clips that do not have screw threads and typically require special tools or techniques for installation. Threaded fasteners are commonly used in applications where disassembly may be required, while non-threaded fasteners are often used in structural or permanent connections. Both types play a crucial role in the aerospace fastener market.</p></p>
