<p><p>Demineralized allografts are commonly used in bone grafting procedures due to their ability to stimulate new bone growth. There are three main types of demineralized allografts on the market: gel, putty, and putty with chips. Gel allografts are in a liquid form, making them easy to inject and conform to the shape of the bone defect. Putty allografts have a thicker consistency, providing more structural support. Putty with chips combines the benefits of putty with added bone chips for enhanced bone regeneration.</p></p>
