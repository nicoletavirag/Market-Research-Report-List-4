<p><p>Low melting fiber market can be categorized into two types based on their melting points: Melting Point ≤130 ℃ and Melting Point >130 ℃. Fibers with a melting point of ≤130 ℃ are suitable for applications where low heat resistance is required, such as in thermal bonding of nonwoven fabrics. On the other hand, fibers with a melting point >130 ℃ are ideal for applications where higher heat resistance is needed, like in automotive interiors or protective clothing. Each type offers specific advantages depending on the intended use.</p></p>
