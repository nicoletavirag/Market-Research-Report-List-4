<p><p>Lipid-lowering agents are medications used to lower cholesterol levels in the body. The market for these agents can be categorized into different types such as Niacin, Acipimox, Fibrates, and others. Niacin works by lowering triglycerides and increasing HDL cholesterol levels. Acipimox is a nicotinic acid derivative that lowers triglycerides and LDL cholesterol. Fibrates are a class of medications that lower triglycerides and increase HDL cholesterol. Other lipid-lowering agents may include statins, bile acid sequestrants, and PCSK9 inhibitors.</p></p>
