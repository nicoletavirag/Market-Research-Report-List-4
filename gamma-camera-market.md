<p><p>A gamma camera, also known as a scintillation camera, is a device used in nuclear medicine for imaging internal organs. It detects gamma rays emitted by radioactive substances administered to the patient, producing images that help doctors diagnose and monitor conditions such as cancer, heart disease, and neurological disorders. The market is witnessing a shift towards digital gamma cameras, which offer higher image resolution, improved sensitivity, and reduced radiation exposure for patients. Additionally, the integration of artificial intelligence and machine learning technology in gamma cameras is expected to drive market growth, as these tools can improve image analysis accuracy and enhance diagnostic capabilities.</p><p>Other trends in the gamma camera market include a growing focus on patient comfort and convenience, with manufacturers developing more compact and mobile gamma cameras that can be used in various healthcare settings. The market is also seeing increased adoption of hybrid imaging systems, such as SPECT/CT and SPECT/MRI, which combine gamma camera imaging with other modalities for more comprehensive diagnostic information. Overall, the gamma camera market is poised for significant growth and innovation in the coming years.</p></p>
