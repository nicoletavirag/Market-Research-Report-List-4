<p><p>Electrical Energy Storage (EES) involves storing electricity for later use. There are different types of EES markets based on the storage medium: physical, electromagnetic, and chemical. Physical storage includes technologies like pumped hydro and compressed air. Electromagnetic storage uses superconducting magnets or inductors to store energy. Chemical storage involves batteries and fuel cells to convert electrical energy into chemical form for later use. Each market type has its own advantages and limitations depending on factors like efficiency, cost, and scalability.</p></p>
