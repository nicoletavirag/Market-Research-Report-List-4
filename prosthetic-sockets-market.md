<p><p>Prosthetic sockets are customized components that connect an artificial limb to the residual limb of an amputee. There are different types of prosthetic sockets, including Transfemoral sockets for above-the-knee amputations and Leg sockets for below-the-knee amputations. Transfemoral sockets are designed to provide support and control for the entire lower limb, while Leg sockets are tailored to fit and support the lower leg. Both types of sockets play a crucial role in ensuring comfort, stability, and mobility for amputees using prosthetic limbs.</p></p>
