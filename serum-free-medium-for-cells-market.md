<p><p>Serum-free medium for cells is a type of culture medium that does not contain serum, which can introduce variability and interference in cell culture experiments. There are two main types of serum-free medium markets: with DMSO and without DMSO. DMSO (dimethyl sulfoxide) is often included in serum-free medium as a cryoprotectant to prevent cell damage during freezing and thawing. Some researchers prefer serum-free medium without DMSO to avoid any potential side effects or interference with their cell culture studies. Both types offer advantages depending on the specific needs of the research or application.</p></p>
