<p><p>DC distribution networks are classified based on voltage levels into three main market types: Low Voltage (LV), Medium Voltage (MV), and High Voltage (HV). LV networks typically operate at voltages below 1 kV and cater to residential and small commercial consumers. MV networks operate at voltages between 1 kV and 50 kV, serving larger commercial and industrial consumers. HV networks operate at voltages above 50 kV and are used for long-distance transmission and high-capacity industrial applications. Each market type has specific equipment and operational requirements to ensure efficient power distribution.</p></p>
