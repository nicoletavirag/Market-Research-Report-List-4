<p><p>Vectorized antibodies are often delivered using various methods such as Adenoassociated Virus (AAV) Vector, Electroporation, Lipid Nanoparticles (LNPs), and others. AAV vectors are commonly used for gene therapy due to their ability to enter target cells and deliver genetic material. Electroporation involves using an electrical field to create temporary pores in cell membranes for antibody delivery. LNPs are lipid-based delivery systems that encapsulate antibodies for targeted delivery. Other methods such as viral vectors and protein-based carriers are also used in the vectorized antibody market.</p></p>
