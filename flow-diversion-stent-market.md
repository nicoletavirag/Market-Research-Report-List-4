<p><p>A flow diversion stent is a type of medical device used to treat intracranial aneurysms by redirecting blood flow away from the aneurysm. It is a highly effective treatment option for complex and wide-neck aneurysms that are difficult to treat with traditional methods such as coiling. The flow diversion stent works by creating a scaffold-like structure across the neck of the aneurysm, promoting the formation of a blood clot that effectively seals off the aneurysm from the surrounding blood vessels.</p><p>The Flow Diversion Stent Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 6.8% during the forecast period due to factors such as increasing prevalence of intracranial aneurysms, growing adoption of minimally invasive treatment methods, and advancements in stent technology. <p><p>Flow diversion stents are used to divert blood flow away from an aneurysm in the brain. There are two main types of flow diversion stents: single channel and dual channel. Single channel stents have a single lumen for blood flow diversion, while dual channel stents have two lumens to further customize the diversion of blood flow. Both types of stents are used in the treatment of intracranial aneurysms, with the dual channel stents offering a higher level of customization and potentially improved outcomes in certain cases.</p></p>
