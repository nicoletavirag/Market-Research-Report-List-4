<p><p>Reverse Shoulder Arthroplasty is a surgical procedure used to address complex shoulder conditions. The two main types of Reverse Shoulder Arthroplasty are Arthroplasty of the Fossa Humerus and Stem Joint Replacement. Arthroplasty of the Fossa Humerus involves replacing the humeral head with a prosthesis that articulates with the glenoid component. Stem Joint Replacement, on the other hand, involves using a stemmed implant to secure the prosthesis in the humeral bone. Both types aim to improve shoulder function and reduce pain in patients with severe shoulder arthritis or rotator cuff dysfunction.</p></p>
